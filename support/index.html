Getting Started

How do I activate the drawing overlay?

After installing ScreenMark, launch the app — you'll see the ScreenMark icon in your menu bar. The default global shortcut to toggle drawing mode is ⌘⇧D. Press it over any app to instantly start drawing on your screen. Press it again to dismiss the overlay. You can customize all shortcuts in ScreenMark → Settings → Shortcuts.

Why does ScreenMark ask for Screen Recording and Accessibility permissions?

Screen Recording is required by macOS for any app that draws on top of or captures the screen. ScreenMark uses this to render its annotation overlay in real time. Your screen content is never stored or transmitted — it's processed entirely on your device.

Accessibility permission allows ScreenMark to register global keyboard shortcuts that work system-wide, even when another app is in focus. Without this, hotkeys would only work when ScreenMark itself is frontmost. We do not log keystrokes — only the configured shortcut combinations are detected.

You can grant both permissions in macOS System Settings → Privacy & Security.

ScreenMark isn't responding to my global shortcuts. What's wrong?

This is almost always an Accessibility permission issue. Please check:

1. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility
2. Make sure ScreenMark is listed and the toggle is on
3. If it's on but not working, toggle it off, wait a few seconds, then toggle it back on
4. Relaunch ScreenMark from the menu bar icon → Quit, then reopen

If shortcuts still don't work, some third-party apps (like Logi Options+, Karabiner-Elements, or Alfred) can intercept the same key combinations. Try changing your ScreenMark shortcuts in Settings to a different combination.

Features

How do I use Freeze & Capture to annotate a frozen screenshot?

Press your Freeze shortcut (default: ⌘⇧F). ScreenMark captures a snapshot of the current display and freezes it as a static background — your live screen is paused. You can then annotate freely with any tool. When you're done, press Export in the overlay toolbar (or use your export shortcut) to save as PDF, PNG, or TIFF to a location of your choice. Press the shortcut again to unfreeze and return to your live display.

Can I reset the whiteboard countdown timer mid-session?

Yes. While the whiteboard is active and a countdown is running, click the timer display in the overlay toolbar to pause it. Click again to resume, or long-press to reset to your configured duration. You can also change the timer duration (5, 10, or 15 minutes) from the whiteboard settings panel, accessible via the gear icon in the whiteboard toolbar. Changes take effect the next time you start a countdown.

How do I export a screen recording as a GIF?

Start a recording via your Recording shortcut (default: ⌘⇧R). When you stop the recording, a Save dialog will appear. In the Format dropdown, choose Animated GIF or Video (MP4). GIF exports are optimized for small file size and web sharing. Note that GIF recordings are capped at 15 fps for file size efficiency — for higher frame rate output, choose video format.

Does ScreenMark work across multiple displays?

Yes. ScreenMark creates an overlay window on every connected display. When you activate a mode (draw, freeze, whiteboard), it applies to the display where your cursor is currently located. You can annotate different content on different displays independently. Freeze & Capture captures only the display where the shortcut was triggered.

Billing & Account

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are managed entirely through Apple. To cancel:

1. Open the App Store on your Mac
2. Click your profile picture (bottom-left), then Account Settings
3. Under Subscriptions, find ScreenMark and click Edit
4. Select Cancel Subscription

You'll retain Pro access until the end of the current billing period. ScreenMark does not have access to your payment information and cannot process refunds directly — please contact Apple for billing issues.

I purchased a Lifetime license. How do I restore it on a new Mac?

Your Lifetime purchase is tied to your Apple ID. On any new Mac, simply:

1. Download ScreenMark from the Mac App Store (signed in with the same Apple ID)
2. Open the app and go to Settings → Pro
3. Tap Restore Purchases

Your Lifetime entitlement will be restored automatically. If you experience any issues, email us at support@screenmark.app with your order confirmation from Apple and we'll help you resolve it.

What's included in the free version of ScreenMark?

The free version of ScreenMark lets you try the core drawing and annotation experience with limited sessions per day — enough to evaluate if it fits your workflow. Full unrestricted access to all features (Freeze & Capture, Whiteboard, Screen Recording, unlimited drawing sessions, custom shortcuts, iCloud sync) requires a Pro subscription or Lifetime purchase.
