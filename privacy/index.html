Privacy Policy

Effective date: January 1, 2025  ·  Last updated: January 2025

🔒

The short version: ScreenMark does not collect, transmit, or sell your personal data. Everything happens on your device. We built this app for presenters who deserve privacy.

1. Overview

ScreenMark ("we", "our", or "the app") is a macOS screen annotation and presentation overlay application developed by an independent developer. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains our data practices — what we collect (almost nothing), what we don't collect (almost everything), and how the app operates.

By using ScreenMark, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the application.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

We want to be explicit about what we do not collect:

  • No personal identifying information (name, email address, phone number, address)
  • No usage analytics or telemetry
  • No crash reports sent to our servers (macOS crash reports remain with Apple)
  • No behavioral tracking or user profiling
  • No advertising identifiers
  • No screen content — your screen captures, annotations, and recordings never leave your device
  • No microphone or camera data
  • No location data
  • No keystroke logging (global shortcuts are handled locally via system APIs)

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

ScreenMark stores a small amount of data exclusively on your device to provide its features:

  • App Preferences: Settings you configure (shortcut keys, color choices, default tool, stroke width, whiteboard timer durations) are stored in macOS UserDefaults, which resides in your local app container. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly enable iCloud sync (see Section 5).
  • Exports: When you export an annotated snapshot or recording, the file is saved to the location you choose on your Mac. ScreenMark does not retain copies of exports.
  • In-App Purchase State: Your subscription or lifetime purchase entitlement is managed exclusively through Apple's StoreKit framework. ScreenMark does not store payment information.

4. Apple Services

ScreenMark is distributed through the Mac App Store and uses certain Apple platform services. These services are governed by Apple's Privacy Policy:

  • Mac App Store: Apple processes your purchase and manages app delivery. Apple may collect analytics related to App Store usage per their own policy.
  • StoreKit 2: Subscription and in-app purchase validation is handled end-to-end by Apple. ScreenMark receives only a verified entitlement status — no payment details.
  • ScreenCaptureKit: The screen capture framework is used entirely on-device to render the overlay. No screen content is transmitted anywhere.

We have no control over and assume no responsibility for Apple's data practices. We encourage you to review Apple's Privacy Policy for details.

5. iCloud Sync (Optional)

ScreenMark includes an optional iCloud sync feature for Pro subscribers. When enabled, your app preferences and settings are synced via Apple's iCloud Key-Value Storage. This means:

  • Your settings are stored in your personal iCloud account, governed by Apple's privacy practices
  • Only preference data (not screen content or recordings) is synced
  • iCloud sync is opt-in only — it is disabled by default
  • You can disable or delete iCloud data at any time from macOS System Settings → Apple ID → iCloud

6. Screen Recording Permission

ScreenMark requests Screen Recording permission to render its overlay on top of your display content. This permission is required by macOS for any app that draws over or captures the screen. Important clarifications:

  • Screen content is processed entirely in memory, on-device, in real time
  • No screen captures are made unless you explicitly trigger Freeze & Capture or Screen Recording
  • Even when recording, the output file is saved only to a location you choose on your local disk
  • ScreenMark never transmits screen content over the network

7. Accessibility & Input Monitoring Permissions

ScreenMark requests the Accessibility permission to register global keyboard shortcuts that work system-wide. This is a standard macOS requirement for apps that need to respond to hotkeys while other apps are in focus. ScreenMark does not log, record, or transmit keystrokes — it only listens for the specific shortcut combinations you configure.

8. Children's Privacy

ScreenMark is not directed at children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at support@screenmark.app.

9. Data Security

Because ScreenMark does not collect or transmit your personal data to external servers, the attack surface for a data breach involving your information is minimal. Your data remains under your control, protected by macOS's built-in security model and sandboxing. Exported files are subject to whatever security you have configured on your Mac.

10. Third-Party Services

ScreenMark does not integrate with any third-party analytics platforms, advertising networks, crash reporting services (e.g., Crashlytics, Sentry), or marketing tools. There are no third-party SDKs embedded in the application beyond Apple's first-party frameworks.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, if the changes are significant, post a notice in the app or on the App Store listing. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of ScreenMark after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

12. Contact

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests related to this Privacy Policy or your data, please reach out:

Email: support@screenmark.app
We aim to respond to all privacy-related inquiries within 5 business days.
